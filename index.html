About Me

I am a PhD Student in the School of Computing at Clemson University, specifically, in the PERSIST Research Lab. where I build and deploy new protocols, tools, software and hardware technologies that enable embedded networked systems to operate maintenance-free for decades.

Research Interests

My research lies at the intercetion of intermittent computing and wireless sensor networks. I investigate and develop new wireless communication technologies for emerging battery-free, energy harvesting devices plagued with frequent and erratic power failures. My research interests span the following areas:

  • Intermittent Computing
  • Embedded Networked Sensor Systems & IoT
  • Edge Computing & Embedded AI
  • Data Science and Engineering
